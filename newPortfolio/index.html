I've always been very adept to learning new things and strive to be someone who is consistently learning. Having a student mentality is what allows me to stay humble and the top of my game.

In 1995 my mother bought our first family computer. I didn't know any better so I figured it was a vending machine... Yup, I stuck nickels and quarters in the floppy drive. After expressing my frustrations of not getting my soda my mother bought a book on hardware repair. My technological journey had just begun. Growing up I focused a lot on design within Photoshop and understanding how to troubleshoot various hardware and software issues on other peoples computers.

Never truly having embraced my inner nerd, I got a door-to-door sales job in my early 20s selling deregulated energy service to small businesses. Every job I've ever worked I put 110% into and made sure my work ethic was unparalleled compared to my co-workers. Needless to say, I got very good at sales. I later got a job at the headquarters of 2nd largest telecommunications broker in the United States. Having won countless awards for my performance I was at the top of the world. I started getting offers for positions exclusively as a sales manager/trainer but there was just one problem, I wanted to make more of an impact than just teaching people how to finagle signatures from strangers.

I've always wanted to become the next mogul, the next real estate developer, the next 'Mr. Money Bags', the next Mark Zuckerberg. I had no serious capital, no "inside scoop" business relationships, and lacked a deep understanding of how the web worked. I tried to work with some developers locally to get my entrepreneurial visions to come to life but I quickly realized nobody would work on them as hard I would. I saw an ad on facebook for a 24 week coding bootcamp at Northwestern University and immediately applied. I am actively looking for a job as a full time web-developer. I'm excited to use my newfound skill as a web-developer with the goal of creating products and services that will cater to the needs of businesses and their clients.

10/22/2018

I find myself pretty lucky to have been taught web-development during the transition period somewhere between ES5 and ES6 in regards to javascript. With the use of const, let, blocked scope, template strings, arrow functions, and a plethora of other new features ES6 is introducing I'm positive that my workflow through the use of improved shorthand will guide my workflow tremendously. Speaking of workflow I am going to start implementing aliases for all boilerplate and repetitive sequence operations as I've heard it can improve a developers productivity on an average of 75%. WOW! That is an considerably significant increase. When paired in conjunction of a 2nd monitor I'm certain most initialization and boilerplate operations will be an absolute breeze.

Lastly I'd like to talk about my work in developing a trading bot for the crypto market. Before learn web development I spend about 5 months learning a language called Pinescript. You can see the documentation on it here. It was made by the good people over at TradingView and has proven to be a very effective tool at creating a boilerplate for trading strategies. It however has a flaw. I've discovered that when stacked up against live or simulated testing it doesn't fare the same results. This is because simulated back-testing done through TradingView shows would be trades based on historical graphs and pricing. For it to be more effective they would need to implement back-testing based on a play-by-play of the growth of the candles body and wicks whether they be up or down.

The trouble is that a strategy might trigger on the close of a candle with relation to price action and looking at prior data it is sometimes difficult to determine whether the close ever crossed a given threshold and it could revert back to a point above or below once the candle is finished. This is why a live log of live trade data is best when conducting back-testing. Essentially what I am building is a live replay of trade data with respect to price action second-by-second in relation to volume. This will allow be to test strategies in a matter of seconds versus waiting weeks to see a final outcome which is going to save me and my future clients a HUGE amount of time money otherwise spent on testing. It's not a final solution as to say that live testing with real funds is the sure way to go but no other platform offers this feature. I can't wait to release it!
